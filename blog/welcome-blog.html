Welcome Blog

Jul 21, 2021

Welcome to the launch of the new website and my first blog post! My name is Nitish Soni of Jain University, Bangalore. I have so much to tell you. Will start by telling you why I created this blog, what you will be seeing more of in my posts, and my overall experience of creating my website.

Why I started my blog?

Since I started learning Web Development, many of my friends have asked me “Why don’t you start creating a Website?!” and here it is so I Created this WebSite just to update blogs and more. So until a few months ago I didn't truly knew what a “blog” was. I never clicked on blog links, I never understood WHY people wrote blogs, I just thought it was a waste of time to even look into it. But I WAS Wrong. After doing much research and making friends with a few bloggers along the way, who have been so kind to offer much support and advice, I have become completely consumed with inspiration to start a blog. I have finally realized all of the benefits that blogging can bring.

What you will be seeing more in my posts?

Web Development will obviously be the main topic of my blog, but get ready because you will be seeing much more about Technical Stuff, and a mixture of my lifestyle. Writing has never been a strong suite of mine and I will tell you, I am no expert on any of this. I may be a “perfectionist” but I am nowhere near perfect. I figure it will be a lot of trial and error so please bear with me on any grammar/spelling mistakes, and please feel free to always share your advice and communicate with me along the way!

Experience Creating this Website.

What can frustrate many people about coding is that you have to speak the computer’s lingo, and you have to speak it well. You have to learn what tags mean what. You have to learn what sections go where. You have to learn vocabulary, grammar and syntax. And it is a demanding language. In Spanish, you can write ‘n’ in place of ‘ñ’ and you will generally be understood, if not giggled at. In code, ‘;’ and ‘:’ are not interchangeable and never will be. Like any language, you have to endure endless mistakes and constantly consult your dictionary while you stumble through the most basic of sentences. It is very easy to give up, but there’s nothing quite like when you type a line unassisted, open Google Chrome and realize you’ve successfully spoken computer.

So it was kinda tough as I had to check after every line of code that am I able to make the computer understand what I want to make. But it was So fun creating this website. Even I learned lots of new things while creating this site. So hopefully You people liked it too. So don't forget to give your valuable feedback in the feedback section.

- Nitish
